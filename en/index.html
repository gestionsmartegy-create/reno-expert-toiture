Photos of your property
📋 What photos to take?

To receive the most accurate estimate possible, send us the following photos. Don't have everything? Send what you have — we'll work with it.

01
Front view of the house
Step back far enough to capture the full facade and entire roof. Take from each side if possible.
💡 Ideal from the sidewalk or street
02
Each slope of the roof
Walk around the house and take a photo of each side — front, back, left side, right side.
💡 4 to 6 photos are sufficient for most homes
03
Details: gutters, flashing, chimney
Photograph the junctions between roof and walls, the chimney perimeter, gutters and roof overhangs.
💡 These areas account for 80% of problems
04
Damaged or leaking areas (if applicable)
If you have lifted shingles, cracks, water stains on the ceiling or areas that appear damaged — take a close-up photo.
💡 Even a blurry photo is useful
05
Attic or roof structure (optional)
If you have access to your attic, a photo of the wood structure (rafters, decking) helps us assess the overall condition.
💡 Not necessary if access is difficult
06
Aerial view (if available)
If you have a drone photo or a Google Maps screenshot of your roof, it is an excellent complement for measuring the area.
💡 Google Maps screenshot accepted
